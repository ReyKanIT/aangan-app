## Slide 2 — The Problem

### 300M Indian families deserve better than WhatsApp chaos

- **300M+ Indian families** manage everything — photos, events, traditions — through chaotic WhatsApp groups full of forwards and spam
- Family photos are **scattered across 12 different phones** — no shared family memory, no searchable archive
- Wedding planning = 47 phone calls + 3 WhatsApp groups + paper invites. **₹50,000 Cr wedding industry, zero digital coordination**
- **Cultural knowledge is dying** — the average 25-year-old doesn't know their gotra, kuldevi, or sutak rules. One generation away from erasure
- **65+ grandparents are locked out** — complex UIs with tiny text, English-only interfaces, no voice input. The family's most important members can't participate
- No app understands the **3-generation Indian joint family** — parents, married children, grandchildren, all under one digital roof

**The gap:** India has apps for dating, food delivery, and payments. But nothing for the most important unit in Indian society — the family.

## Slide 4 — The Dadi Test (दादी टेस्ट)

### Our north star: If Dadi can use it, everyone can

Every screen in Aangan passes the Dadi Test — our proprietary UX standard for elder-friendly design:

| Dadi Test Rule | Implementation |
|----------------|---------------|
| **Buttons 52px+** | Every tap target is thumb-friendly. No tiny icons |
| **Text 16px+ body** | Large, readable Hindi text. No squinting |
| **Hindi-first labels** | Primary language is Hindi. English subtitles below |
| **Voice-first input** | Speak instead of type. One tap to start talking |
| **Minimal navigation** | 3 tabs, not 7. Every feature within 2 taps |
| **Cultural context** | Panchang on home screen. Festivals front and center |

**Result:** Aangan is the only family app in India where a 70-year-old grandmother in a UP village can post a photo, send a voice message, and check today's tithi — all by herself.
